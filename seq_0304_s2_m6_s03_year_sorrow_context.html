The Year of Sorrow ('Ām al-Ḥuzn)

Context: The 10th Year of Prophethood
The End of the Boycott
Just as the grueling three-year boycott of Banū Hāshim ended, bringing hope for relief, the Prophet ﷺ faced his greatest personal trials. The lifting of the siege did not bring ease, but rather preceded a double tragedy that would shake the foundations of his support in Makkah.

Loss of External Protection

The death of his uncle, Abū Ṭālib, stripped the Prophet ﷺ of his political and tribal immunity. The leadership of Banū Hāshim passed to Abū Lahab, an avowed enemy.

Loss of Internal Comfort

The death of his beloved wife, Khadījah (ra), removed his emotional anchor and primary source of reassurance. She had been his confidante since the very first revelation.
