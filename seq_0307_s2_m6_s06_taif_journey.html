The Journey to Ṭā'if

Seeking a New Sanctuary
Location: ~60 miles SE of Makkah
Tribe: Thaqīf
Companion: Zayd b. Ḥārithah

The Strategic Objective

  • Political Asylum: With Makkah hostile, the Prophet ﷺ sought jiwār (protection) from Thaqīf, the second most powerful tribe in Arabia.
  • A New Base: Ṭā'if was a fertile, fortified city that could serve as a launchpad for the Message, rivaling the religious authority of Quraysh.
  • Direct Appeal: He approached the three chieftains of Thaqīf (Abd Yālīl and his brothers) directly, hoping for a top-down conversion.

The Brutal Reception

  • Arrogant Mockery: The leaders ridiculed him: "Could Allah not find anyone better than you to send?" and "I would tear the cloth of the Ka'bah if you are a Prophet."
  • Mob Violence: They incited slaves and children to line the streets and stone him. He was pelted for three miles until his shoes filled with blood.
  • Zayd's Defense: Zayd b. Ḥārithah used his own body as a shield, suffering severe head wounds to protect the Prophet ﷺ.
This was described by the Prophet ﷺ later to 'Ā'ishah as the "hardest day" of his entire life, exceeding even Uḥud.
