Maximum Danger

"[Remember] when they came at you from above you and from below you, and when eyes shifted [in fear], and hearts reached the throats, and you assumed about Allāh [various] assumptions."

Surah Al-Aḥzāb (33:10)

The Vulnerable Rear

With the men at the trench facing the Confederates ("from above"), the betrayal of Banū Qurayẓah ("from below") meant the fortresses housing women and children were now the front line, completely exposed to attack.

Defense of the Fortress

A Jewish scout breached the fortress of Fāri'. With no men available to defend them, Ṣafiyyah bint 'Abd al-Muṭṭalib (the Prophet's aunt) took a pole, went down, and struck him dead herself.

Her bravery deceived the enemy into thinking the fortress was garrisoned by soldiers.
