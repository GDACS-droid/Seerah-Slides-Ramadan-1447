Zayd's Choice: Implications

The Ultimate Testimony of Character
The Human Proof
A man is truly known by how he treats the powerless. Zayd was a slave, yet he found more dignity, love, and fatherhood with Muḥammad ﷺ than with his own biological family.
The Cultural Shock
In a society obsessed with Nasab (lineage), Zayd's rejection of his noble roots for the Prophet's company was a radical subversion of Arabian values.
The Reciprocation
The Prophet ﷺ immediately responded to this loyalty by taking Zayd to the Ḥijr (Ka'bah) and declaring: "Witness that Zayd is my son; I inherit from him and he inherits from me."
زَيْدُ بْنُ مُحَمَّد Zayd ibn Muḥammad (His name until revelation)
