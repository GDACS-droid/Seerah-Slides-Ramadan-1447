Al-Isrā' wa al-Mi'rāj: Context

Divine Comfort After Earthly Rejection

Al-Isrā' (The Night Journey)

The miraculous horizontal journey by night from Al-Masjid Al-Haram in Makkah to Al-Masjid Al-Aqsa in Jerusalem.

Al-Mi'rāj (The Ascension)

The vertical ascension from Jerusalem through the seven heavens to the Sidrat al-Muntaha (Lote Tree of the Utmost Boundary).

"Exalted is He who took His Servant by night from al-Masjid al-Haram to al-Masjid al-Aqsa... to show him of Our signs." (Surah Al-Isra 17:1)
Theological Purpose: A personal gift to honor the Prophet ﷺ after the "Year of Sorrow" and the rejection at Ṭā'if, confirming his high status with Allah.
