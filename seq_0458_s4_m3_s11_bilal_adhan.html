Bilāl on the Kaaba

The Triumph of Faith over Lineage

The Historic Command

The Prophet ﷺ ordered Bilāl ibn Rabāḥ, a former Abyssinian slave who had been tortured in Makkah for his faith, to climb the roof of the Kaaba—the holiest site in Arabia—to call the Adhān.

The Symbolism

This act physically and symbolically placed a former slave above the chiefs of Quraysh, dismantling the idols of racism and class superiority alongside the idols of stone.
