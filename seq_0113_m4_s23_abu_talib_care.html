Abū Ṭālib's Care

More Than a Father
Domestic Tenderness
  • He would not eat unless the young Muḥammad ﷺ was present.
  • He made him sleep next to his own bed for comfort and protection.
  • He favored him over his own biological children in food and attention.
Public Protection
  • He took him to public gatherings and tribal councils, integrating him into society.
  • He defended him fiercely against any perceived slight or harm.
  • He later stood as a political shield against the entirety of Quraysh.
