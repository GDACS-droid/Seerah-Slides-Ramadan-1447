The Incident of 'Addās

A Glimmer of Light in the Darkness
Taking shelter in a garden owned by 'Utbah and Shaybah (sons of Rabī'ah), the Prophet ﷺ was offered grapes by their Christian slave, 'Addās.
1
The Bismillah
Before eating, the Prophet ﷺ said "Bismillah" (In the name of Allah). 'Addās was surprised: "The people of this land do not say these words."
2
The Connection
The Prophet ﷺ asked his origin. 'Addās replied, "Nineveh." The Prophet ﷺ smiled: "The city of the righteous man Yūnus bin Mattā (Jonah)."
3
The Brotherhood
'Addās asked how he knew Yūnus. The Prophet ﷺ replied: "He is my brother; he was a Prophet and I am a Prophet."
4
The Recognition
Overcome with emotion, 'Addās kissed the Prophet's ﷺ head, hands, and feet, recognizing the signs of Prophethood that the arrogant chiefs ignored.
The Divine Wisdom
While the powerful chieftains of Thaqīf rejected the message with arrogance, a humble slave from a distant land accepted the truth immediately.
