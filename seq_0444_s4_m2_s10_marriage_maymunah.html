Marriage to Maymūnah bint al-Ḥārith

The Final Union & Strategic Diplomacy

The Union

Maymūnah was the sister of Umm al-Faḍl (wife of Al-'Abbās). The marriage was proposed by Al-'Abbās during the 'Umrah.

She holds the distinction of being the last woman the Prophet ﷺ married.

"She surrendered herself to the Prophet."

Strategic Impact

Alliance with Najd: She belonged to the powerful Banū Hilāl tribe. This union helped neutralize the hostility of the Najdi tribes (Hawāzin) and soften their hearts towards Islam.

Attempted Reconciliation: The Prophet ﷺ invited the Quraysh to the wedding feast (Walīmah) to build bridges, but they rudely refused: "We have no need for your food. Leave us."

Sarif: The location just outside Makkah where the marriage was consummated, and coincidentally, where she passed away and was buried years later.
