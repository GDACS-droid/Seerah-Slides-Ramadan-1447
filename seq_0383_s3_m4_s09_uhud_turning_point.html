The Turning Point

The Fatal Mistake

Seeing the Meccans retreat, 40 of the 50 archers abandoned their post to collect spoils, ignoring their commander 'Abdullāh ibn Jubayr.

"The people are looting! What are you waiting for?"
Khālid ibn al-Walīd

The Tactical Opportunity

Commanding the Meccan cavalry, Khālid noticed the rear flank was now exposed. He wheeled his forces around Mount Uḥud, killed the remaining 10 archers, and launched a devastating attack from behind.

The Pincer Movement: The Muslims were now trapped between the returning Meccan infantry and Khālid's cavalry.
