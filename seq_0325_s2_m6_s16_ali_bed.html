Ali in the Prophet's Bed

The Ultimate Act of Trust
"And We have put before them a barrier and behind them a barrier and covered them, so they do not see."
Surah Yā-Sīn (36:9)

The Decoy

The Prophet ﷺ instructed his young cousin, 'Alī b. Abī Ṭālib (ra), to sleep in his bed covered with his green mantle. This served two purposes:
  • To deceive the assassins peering through the cracks, making them think the Prophet ﷺ was still sleeping.
  • To return the trusts (Amānāt) that the people of Makkah had left with the Prophet ﷺ, even though they were plotting to kill him.

The Escape

While 11 assassins (including Abū Jahl) surrounded the house, the Prophet ﷺ emerged reciting Surah Yā-Sīn.
He threw dust on their heads, and by Allah's will, their vision was blocked. He walked right past them unharmed and headed to the house of Abū Bakr to begin the Hijrah.
