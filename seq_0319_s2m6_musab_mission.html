Muṣ'ab ibn 'Umayr in Yathrib

السَّفِير الأَوَّل — The First Ambassador (621–622 CE)
The Mission
Muṣ'ab stayed with As'ad ibn Zurārah (a Khazraj leader and early convert). He taught the Qur'ān, led prayers, and quietly built a Muslim community household by household.
He also led the first Jumu'ah prayer in Yathrib — before the Prophet ﷺ ever arrived — with approximately 40 worshippers.
The Breakthrough: Sa'd ibn Mu'ādh
Usayd ibn Ḥuḍayr (Aws leader) was sent to confront Muṣ'ab but converted after hearing the Qur'ān. He then convinced Sa'd ibn Mu'ādh, the chief of Banū 'Abd al-Ashhal (Aws), to listen.
Sa'd converted — and his entire clan followed him that same day. This was the tipping point for Islam in Yathrib.
Within one year, Islam had entered virtually every household in Yathrib. Muṣ'ab returned to Makkah at the next Hajj season with 75 new Muslims ready to pledge allegiance.
