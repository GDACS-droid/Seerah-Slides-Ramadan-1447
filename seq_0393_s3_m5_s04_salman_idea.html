The Persian Strategy

The Vulnerability

Madīnah was naturally protected on the East and West by impassable volcanic lava fields (Ḥarrah) and on the South by dense palm groves. The North, however, was open terrain—a fatal weakness against 10,000 soldiers.

Salmān al-Fārisī

A seeker of truth who had traveled from Persia to Syria to Madīnah. He possessed knowledge of siege warfare techniques that were completely unknown to the Arabs of the Peninsula.

The Trench (Khandaq)

"O Messenger of Allāh, in Persia, when we were besieged, we would dig a trench around us."

This defensive barrier would neutralize the enemy cavalry and force a stalemate, preventing the city from being overrun.
