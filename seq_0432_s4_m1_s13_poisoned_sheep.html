The Poisoned Sheep

Assassination Attempt & Divine Protection

The Plot

Zaynab bint al-Ḥārith, a Jewish woman of Khaybar, asked which part of the sheep the Prophet ﷺ preferred. Upon learning it was the shoulder, she infused it with a lethal dose of poison.

"If he is a king, I will rid us of him. If he is a Prophet, he will be informed."

— Zaynab's Reasoning

The Miracle

The Prophet ﷺ took one bite but did not swallow. He immediately ordered his Companions: "Lift your hands! This shoulder tells me it is poisoned."

The Victim

Bishr ibn al-Barā' had already swallowed his bite. The poison was so potent that he died shortly after, turning purple.

Long-term Impact
The Prophet ﷺ felt the pain of that poison resurface during his final illness years later, granting him the station of a Shahīd (Martyr) in addition to Prophethood.
