The Ḥunafā': Exceptions to the Rule

Remnants of the Ibrahimic Tradition
حنيف
Ḥanīf (Singular)
حنفاء
Ḥunafā' (Plural)
Linguistic Meaning

From the root Ḥ-N-F, meaning "to incline" or "to turn away" — one who turns from crookedness toward the straight path.

Historical Context

Solitary figures who rejected Quraysh paganism and sought remnants of the Religion of Ibrāhīm.

Theological Significance
Their existence shows the Fiṭrah (innate disposition) was buried but not extinguished in Jāhiliyyah.
