The Dua of Ṭā'if

"To You I Complain of My Weakness..."
"O Allah, to You I complain of my weakness, my lack of resources, and my humiliation before the people. O Most Merciful of those who show mercy! You are the Lord of the weak, and You are my Lord... If You are not angry with me, then I do not care; but Your protection is more encompassing for me."
"I seek refuge in the Light of Your Face by which darkness is illuminated... that Your anger should descend upon me... I seek Your forgiveness until You are pleased."
Complaint To Allah, Not Of Allah
The Prophet ﷺ expresses his vulnerability ("Ashku...") to his Creator, not questioning Allah's decree but seeking His strength.
The Ultimate Concern
"If You are not angry with me, I do not care." His only fear was that this trial might be a sign of Divine displeasure.
The Angel of Mountains: Offered to crush Ṭā'if between two mountains. The Prophet ﷺ refused: "No, I hope that Allah will bring forth from their loins those who worship Allah alone."
