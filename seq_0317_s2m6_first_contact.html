First Contact: Six Men from Khazraj

Year 11 of Prophethood (620 CE) — Hajj Season at 'Aqabah
During the Hajj season, the Prophet ﷺ continued his practice of approaching visiting tribes. At the ravine of 'Aqabah near Minā, he met six men from the Khazraj of Yathrib.
When they heard his message, they recognized the description of the prophet that the Jewish tribes had long warned them about. They said to one another:
"By Allah, this is the prophet the Jews have been threatening us with. Let us not allow them to get to him before us!"
They accepted Islam on the spot and returned to Yathrib, spreading the message. By the next Hajj season, Islam had reached every major clan in the city.
The Jewish Factor
The Jewish tribes of Yathrib had spoken of a coming prophet from their scriptures. When the Arabs of Yathrib heard Muḥammad's message, they connected it immediately to what the Jews had described — and they wanted to be first to follow him.
