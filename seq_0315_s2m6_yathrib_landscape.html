Yathrib: The Political Landscape

يَثْرِب — An Oasis of Tribal Rivalries (~450 km north of Makkah)
Al-Aws
Settled in the south and southeast of the oasis. Allied with Banū Qurayẓah (Jewish). Descendants of Qayla, originally from Yemen after the collapse of the Ma'rib Dam.
Al-Khazraj
Settled in the north and center. Allied with Banū Qaynuqā' and Banū Naḍīr (Jewish). Larger in number, but weakened by decades of war with Al-Aws.
Jewish Tribes
Banū Qaynuqā' (goldsmiths, central market), Banū Naḍīr (date farmers, south), Banū Qurayẓah (south). Held scriptural knowledge and economic influence.
No central authority. Yathrib had no equivalent of Quraysh's Dār al-Nadwah. Tribal alliances shifted constantly, and blood feuds persisted for generations. The Jewish tribes had long told the Arabs of a coming prophet from their scriptures.
