The Pursuit: Ḥamrā' al-Asad

Sunday, 8th Shawwāl (The day after the battle)

The March of the Wounded

Despite severe wounds and exhaustion, the Prophet ﷺ ordered an immediate pursuit of the enemy. He stipulated that only those who fought at Uḥud could participate.

Objective: To project strength and deter Quraysh from returning to finish off Madīnah.

Psychological Warfare

Ma'bad al-Khuzā'ī (a sympathetic ally) met Abu Sufyān and exaggerated the Muslim strength: "Muhammad is coming with an army the likes of which I have never seen, burning with rage."

Result: Terrified by the news, Abu Sufyān abandoned his plan to attack Madīnah and fled to Makkah.
Strategic Victory: The operation transformed a military defeat into a psychological stalemate, preserving the State's reputation.
