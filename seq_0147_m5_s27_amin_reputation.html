Al-Amīn: Pre-Revelation Reputation

The Foundation of Credibility
الأَمِين
A title unanimously bestowed by his society, not claimed by himself. It signified absolute integrity in an era of tribal partisanship.

The Keeper of Trusts (Amānāt)

Even after he began preaching Islam, the pagans of Quraysh continued to deposit their valuables with him for safekeeping, trusting no one else.

Truthful Speech (Al-Ṣādiq)

In 40 years, he was never known to tell a lie, neither in serious matters nor in jest. His tongue was known for purity.

Moral Consistency

His private life matched his public persona. There was no duality, no hidden vices, and no history of youthful indiscretions common to his peers.

"He would not refrain from lying about people and then go and lie about Allah."
The Logic of Heraclius (Roman Emperor) questioning Abu Sufyan
الصَّادِقُ الأَمِين Al-Ṣādiq al-Amīn (The Truthful, The Trustworthy)
