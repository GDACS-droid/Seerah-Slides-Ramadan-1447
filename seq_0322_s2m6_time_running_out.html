Time Running Out in Makkah

دَار النَّدْوَة — Quraysh's Final Council
The Exodus Noticed
As Muslims left in small groups, Quraysh realized their community was emptying. Homes stood abandoned. The economic and social fabric of Makkah was being disrupted. Some families were forcibly separated — Umm Salamah was prevented from leaving with her husband and child.
Quraysh's Desperation
With the Second Pledge of 'Aqabah, Quraysh understood that the Prophet ﷺ now had a military alliance and a safe haven. If he reached Yathrib, he would be beyond their control. The threat was existential — to their trade, their religion, and their political order.
The Assassination Plot — Dār al-Nadwah
Quraysh convened an emergency council. Abū Jahl proposed that one young man from each clan strike the Prophet ﷺ simultaneously, so that no single tribe could be held responsible and Banū Hāshim could not seek blood revenge against all of Quraysh. Allah revealed the plot: "And when those who disbelieved plotted against you..." (Q 8:30)
