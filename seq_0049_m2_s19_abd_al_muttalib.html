'Abd al-Muṭṭalib

The Master of Quraysh (Sayyid Quraysh)
الفياض
Al-Fayyāḍ
"The Overflowing One"

A title earned for his unmatched generosity in feeding both pilgrims and the poor of Makkah.

Uncontested Leadership

After the death of his uncle Al-Muṭṭalib, he consolidated power, becoming the sole voice of authority in Makkah.

Feeder of Men and Beasts

He was known to feed people in the plains and even leave food for wild beasts and birds on the mountain tops.

Moral Rectitude

He revived many Abrahamic traditions: prohibiting wine, punishing adultery, and honoring the Sacred Months.

"He was the most handsome, most eloquent, and most forbearing of the Arabs."
