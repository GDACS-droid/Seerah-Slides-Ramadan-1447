The Judgment of Sa'd

The Arbiter

Banū Qurayẓah refused the Prophet's judgment and specifically requested Sa'd ibn Mu'ādh, the leader of Aws (their former allies), hoping for leniency.

Sa'd was brought on a donkey, mortally wounded from an arrow at the Trench. He made his people swear to abide by his decree.

The Verdict

"I judge that the fighting men be killed, their wealth divided, and their women and children taken as captives."

The Prophet ﷺ said: "You have judged them with the judgment of the King (Allāh) from above the seven heavens."
Historical Context: This judgment for treason was consistent with their own law in the Torah (Deuteronomy 20:12-13): "...you shall strike every male thereof with the edge of the sword."
