The Threat of Hawāzin

A Reckless Mobilization

Mālik ibn 'Awf

A young, charismatic, but reckless leader of the Hawāzin tribe (30 years old). He refused to accept the rise of Quraysh and Islam.

The Alliance:
Hawāzin + Thaqīf (Ṭā'if)
(Arch-rivals of Quraysh)

The "All-In" Strategy

Mālik ordered the army to bring their women, children, and livestock to the battlefield. His logic: Men will fight to the death if their families and wealth are behind them.

"You sheep-herder! Do you think anything can stop a defeated man from fleeing? If you win, you win with swords and spears. But if you lose, you will be disgraced in your family and wealth."
— Warning of Durayd ibn al-Simmah (The Blind Veteran)
