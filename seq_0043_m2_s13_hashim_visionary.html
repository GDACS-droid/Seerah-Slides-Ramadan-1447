Hāshim ibn 'Abd Manāf

The Economic Visionary
Real Name: 'Amr al-'Ulā
هاشم
Etymology: "The Crusher"
From Hashama (to crush/break). He earned this title by crushing bread into broth (Tharīd) to feed his starving people during a famine.

  • Custodian of Hospitality

    He inherited the Siqāyah (Watering) and Rifādah (Feeding) offices, transforming them into a systematic operation.

  • Legendary Generosity

    He spent his own wealth to ensure no pilgrim went hungry, setting the standard for Qurayshī leadership as service.

"'Amr who crushed bread for his people in Makkah
when they were starving and lean."
Ancient Arab Poetry
