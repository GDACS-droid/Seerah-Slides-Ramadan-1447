Burial & Succession

The Sacred & The Political

The Sacred Rites

The Washing (Ghusl) Performed by his closest family (Ali, Abbas, and his sons) without removing his shirt, out of respect for his sanctity.
The Burial Site A debate arose on where to bury him. Abū Bakr resolved it with a Hadith:
"Prophets are buried where they die."

He was buried in the exact spot where his soul departed, in Aisha's room.

The Succession

Saqīfah Bani Sa'idah The Anṣār gathered to choose a leader. Abū Bakr and Umar rushed to the gathering to prevent a split between Anṣār and Muhājirūn.
The Pledge (Bay'ah) After intense debate, Umar took the hand of Abū Bakr and pledged allegiance. The Anṣār followed suit.
Abū Bakr al-Ṣiddīq became the first Caliph, preserving the unity of the Ummah.
The Prophet ﷺ was not buried until the political unity of the Muslims was secured.
