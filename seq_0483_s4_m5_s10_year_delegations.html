The Year of Delegations

Sanat al-Wufūd (9 AH)

Thaqīf (Ṭā'if)

The former enemies who once stoned the Prophet ﷺ sent a delegation to accept Islam, asking only that their idol (Al-Lāt) not be destroyed by their own hands. The Prophet ﷺ sent Abū Sufyān and Mughīrah to destroy it.

Banū Tamīm

The desert poets challenged the Muslims to a contest of oratory and poetry. After hearing the Quran and the speeches of the Companions, they admitted defeat and embraced Islam.

Christians of Najrān

Engaged in theological debate about Jesus (Isa AS). When challenged to Mubahalah (mutual invocation of curse), they declined and opted for a peace treaty and payment of Jizyah instead.

Banū Ḥanīfah

The tribe of Musaylimah. They accepted Islam, but Musaylimah later claimed prophethood and demanded a share in the authority, sowing the seeds of future conflict.

وَرَأَيْتَ النَّاسَ يَدْخُلُونَ فِي دِينِ اللَّهِ أَفْوَاجًا
"And you see the people entering into the religion of Allah in multitudes..." (110:2)
