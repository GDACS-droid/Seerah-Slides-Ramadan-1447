The Religious Economy

The Symbiosis of Faith and Trade
The Ka'bah
The spiritual magnet that attracted tribes from all over Arabia for Hajj.
The Sanctuary (Ḥaram)
A zone of absolute peace. Blood feuds were suspended, ensuring safe passage.
The Markets (Sūq)
Trade fairs like 'Ukāẓ flourished because enemies could trade side-by-side safely.
"The Neutral Ground"
Makkah became the Switzerland of Arabia—wealth built on neutrality.
"Have they not seen that We made a safe sanctuary, while people are being snatched away all around them?"
Sūrah Al-'Ankabūt (29:67)
