The Second Pledge of 'Aqabah

بَيْعَة العَقَبَة الثَّانِيَة — Dhul Ḥijjah, Year 13 (622 CE)
The Setting
73 men and 2 women came secretly at night during Hajj to the ravine of 'Aqabah. Al-'Abbās ibn 'Abd al-Muṭṭalib (the Prophet's uncle, not yet Muslim) attended as a witness and guarantor of the seriousness of the commitment.
The Terms (Bay'at al-Ḥarb)
To hear and obey in ease and hardship
To spend in plenty and in want
To enjoin good and forbid evil
To fear no blame in Allah's cause
To protect the Prophet ﷺ as they protect their own families and children
Key Figures
Al-Barā' ibn Ma'rūr was the first to extend his hand. The 2 women were Nusaybah bint Ka'b (later fought at Uḥud) and Asmā' bint 'Amr.
12 Naqībs Appointed
The Prophet ﷺ appointed 12 representatives (Naqībs) — 9 from Khazraj, 3 from Aws — as tribal guarantors, modeled on the 12 chiefs of the Israelites (Q 5:12).
Outcome
This was a political and military alliance. The Hijrah to Yathrib was now mandated. Quraysh discovered the pledge and attempted to stop the delegates, but they escaped.
"Blood is blood, and destruction is destruction. You are of me and I am of you." — Prophet Muḥammad ﷺ to the Anṣār
