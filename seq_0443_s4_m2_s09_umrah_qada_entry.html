'Umrah al-Qaḍā': The Return

Fulfilling the Vision of Victory

The Entry

Exactly one year after the Treaty of Ḥudaybiyyah, 2,000 Muslims entered Makkah with their weapons sheathed, fulfilling the divine promise: "You shall certainly enter the Sacred Mosque..."

The Show of Strength

To disprove the Quraysh's rumor that the "fever of Yathrib" had weakened them, the Prophet ﷺ ordered the men to perform Ramal (brisk walking with chests out) during the first three rounds of Ṭawāf.

Lā ilāha ill-Allāh waḥdah
Naṣara 'abdah
Wa a'azza jundah
Wa hazama al-aḥzāba waḥdah
"There is no god but Allah alone. He fulfilled His promise, helped His servant, granted might to His soldiers, and defeated the Confederates alone."
The View from Mount Qu'ayq'ān: The leaders of Quraysh watched from the hills, expecting to see a weak, emaciated group. Instead, they saw a disciplined, powerful force thundering the Talbiyah.
