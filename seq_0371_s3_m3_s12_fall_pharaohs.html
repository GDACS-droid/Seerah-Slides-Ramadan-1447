The Fall of the "Pharaohs"

Abu Jahl
The Arch-Enemy

Fatally wounded by two young Anṣārī boys, Mu'ādh and Mu'awwidh, who sought him out to avenge his insults to the Prophet ﷺ.

Poetic Justice
The "Pharaoh of this Ummah," arrogant in his power, was brought down by the youngest and least experienced warriors.
Umayyah ibn Khalaf
The Torturer

Spotted by Bilāl ibn Rabāḥ, his former slave whom he had tortured in Makkah. Bilāl called out, "The head of disbelief! I shall not survive if he survives!"

Poetic Justice
The master who claimed absolute ownership over the slave's life was killed by the very man he sought to crush.
Collapse of Leadership: 24 chieftains of Quraysh fell, creating a power vacuum eventually filled by Abu Sufyān.
