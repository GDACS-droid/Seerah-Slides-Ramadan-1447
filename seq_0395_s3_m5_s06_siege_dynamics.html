The Standoff

"By Allāh, this is a stratagem that the Arabs have never employed!"
The Confederates arrived expecting an open field battle, only to be halted by the massive trench. Their cavalry was rendered useless, and they were forced to camp in the cold desert.

The Deadlock

For nearly a month (27 days), the two armies exchanged arrows but could not engage in full combat.

The Muslims patrolled the trench night and day, enduring bitter cold and hunger, while the Confederates grew frustrated as their supplies dwindled.

The Duel

A few horsemen found a narrow point and jumped the trench. 'Amr ibn 'Abd Wudd, a legendary warrior said to be equal to 1,000 men, challenged the Muslims.

'Alī ibn Abī Ṭālib VS 'Amr ibn 'Abd Wudd

'Alī accepted the challenge. Despite 'Amr's size and experience, 'Alī struck him down with a decisive blow, shouting "Allāhu Akbar!" The other intruders fled back across the trench.
