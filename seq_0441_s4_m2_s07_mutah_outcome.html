Strategic Analysis: Victory or Defeat?

Breaking the Prestige of Rome

The Surface View

Some in Madīnah initially viewed the retreat as a failure, calling the returning army "Al-Furrār" (The Fleers) because they did not conquer the land or defeat the enemy army completely.

The objective of avenging the envoy was not fully met in the traditional sense of occupation.

The Strategic Reality

Survival as Victory: For 3,000 men to engage 200,000 and return with only 12 martyrs is a military miracle and a tactical masterclass.

Shattering the Myth: The battle proved to the Arab tribes that Rome was not invincible. It broke the psychological barrier of fighting a superpower.

Prestige: The Romans were shocked by the ferocity of the Muslims, deterring them from immediate invasion.

The Prophetic Verdict

"They are not fleers (Al-Furrār), but they are the returners/attackers (Al-Kurrār), if Allāh wills."
