Why One Man Could Change an Entire Religion

Three Factors Behind the Success of 'Amr's Innovation
01

Inferiority Complex

عقدة النقص

'Amr was overawed by the Amalekites' military power, architecture, and advanced civilization. He assumed their material success meant their theology was correct.

02

Personal Authority

زعامة

As the chieftain of Khuzā'ah, 'Amr was wealthy, generous, and a respected war hero. His people followed him due to his prestige and credentials.

03

Temporal Distance

بُعد زمني

Approximately 2,000 years had passed since Ibrāhīm (AS). No prophetic guidance had intervened, allowing ignorance to prevail and traditions to fade.
