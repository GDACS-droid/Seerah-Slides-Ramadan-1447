The Negotiations

Quraysh's Envoys

Budayl ibn Warqā' (Khuzā'ah)
Confirmed the Muslims came only for 'Umrah, with no weapons of war.
Ḥulays ibn 'Alqamah (Bedouin Chief)
Saw the sacrificial animals garlanded and honored them, returning to scold Quraysh for blocking pilgrims.
'Urwah ibn Mas'ūd (Thaqīf)
The astute diplomat sent to assess the psychological state of the Muslims.

The Observation

"O people of Quraysh! I have visited Chosroes in his kingdom, and Caesar in his kingdom, and the Negus in his kingdom. By Allāh, I have never seen a king honored by his people as Muhammad is honored by his companions."

— 'Urwah's Report to Quraysh
The Incident: During talks, 'Urwah tried to touch the Prophet's beard (a custom). Mughīrah ibn Shu'bah struck his hand with a sword hilt, saying: "Keep your hand away from the face of the Messenger of Allāh."
