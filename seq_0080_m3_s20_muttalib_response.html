'Abd al-Muṭṭalib's Response

The Meeting with Abrahah
The Context: Abrahah's army had seized 200 camels belonging to 'Abd al-Muṭṭalib. When the Qurayshi chief entered the camp, his dignity was such that Abrahah descended from his throne to sit with him on the carpet.
Abrahah
"I was impressed by you, but now I withdraw my respect. I came to destroy your House—your religion and honor—and you speak to me about 200 camels?"
'Abd al-Muṭṭalib
"I am the lord of the camels. As for the House, it has a Lord who will protect it."
أَنَا رَبُّ الإِبِلِ، وَلِلْبَيْتِ رَبٌّ يَحْمِيهِ
"I am the lord of the camels, and the House has a Lord who will protect it."
A statement of ultimate Tawakkul (Reliance) — acknowledging human limits and Divine power.
