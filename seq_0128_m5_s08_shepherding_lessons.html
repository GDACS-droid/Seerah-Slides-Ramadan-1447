Lessons of Shepherding

Why Shepherding Before Prophethood?
Patience (Ṣabr)
Sheep are difficult to herd and scatter constantly. Keeping them together requires immense patience and gentle persistence.
Leadership
Managing a flock teaches how to care for a community, recognizing the weak, the stray, and the stubborn.
Solitude
Long hours alone in nature foster deep contemplation and spiritual reflection, away from the noise of the city.
Humility
It is humble labor that strips away pride. The leader serves the flock, not the other way around.
Vigilance
The shepherd must be constantly alert to predators, just as a Prophet must guard his community from spiritual dangers.
الصَّبْر Al-Ṣabr (Patience)
