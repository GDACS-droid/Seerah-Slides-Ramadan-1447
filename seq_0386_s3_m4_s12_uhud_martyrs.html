The Martyrs (Shuhadā')

Ḥamzah ibn 'Abd al-Muṭṭalib
Sayyid al-Shuhadā' (Master of Martyrs)

Targeted by Waḥshī (an Abyssinian slave) with a spear, promised freedom by Hind bint 'Utbah for the killing.

His body was brutally mutilated; Hind cut open his chest and attempted to chew his liver in an act of savage vengeance for her father at Badr.

Muṣ'ab ibn 'Umayr
The Standard Bearer

Refused to let the flag fall even after losing both hands. His physical resemblance to the Prophet ﷺ led his killer to shout, "I have killed Muḥammad!" causing panic.

Once the wealthiest youth of Makkah, he died with only a short shroud; if his head was covered, his feet were exposed and covered with grass (Idhkhir).

The Tragedy of Mutilation (Muthlah)

The women of Quraysh entered the battlefield, cutting off ears and noses of the martyrs to make necklaces. The Prophet ﷺ was deeply pained by the sight of his uncle Ḥamzah but forbade Muslims from retaliating in kind.
