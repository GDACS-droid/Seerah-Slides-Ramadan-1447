The Prophet's Houses

حجرات النبي

Adjacent to the mosque, simple rooms were built for the Prophet's family. Initially, two were constructed (for Sawdah and 'Ā'ishah), reflecting the ultimate asceticism of the leader of the state.

Materials

Palm branches covered with mud; doors were merely curtains of hair cloth.

Height

The ceilings were low enough that a standing person could touch them.

Access

Opened directly into the mosque, integrating his private life with his public mission.

"I entered the houses of the Messenger of Allah... and I could touch the ceiling with my hand."

Al-Hasan al-Basri (Describing them before demolition)
