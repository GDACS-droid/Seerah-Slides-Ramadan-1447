The Fulfillment of the Vow

'Abd al-Muṭṭalib's Ten Sons

Decades earlier, he had vowed to sacrifice one son if God granted him ten. Now, the sons stood before him as grown men—strong, noble, and protectors of their father.

النَّذْر
Al-Nadhr (The Solemn Vow)
"If You grant me ten sons who grow to maturity to protect me, I will sacrifice one of them for You at the Ka'bah."
Cultural Context: In pre-Islamic Arabia, a Nadhr was binding. Breaking it was unthinkable, believed to invite immediate divine wrath and social shame.
"A test of faith echoing the trial of his forefather Ibrāhīm."
