The Geopolitical Context

Yemen Under Abyssinian Rule
The Aksumite Rule
  • Yemen was a vassal state of the powerful Aksumite Empire (modern-day Ethiopia).
  • Ruled by the viceroy Abrahah al-Ashram, a formidable Christian general who had seized power.
  • Abrahah sought to consolidate power and spread Christianity across the Arabian Peninsula.
The Rival Sanctuary
  • Abrahah built a magnificent cathedral in Ṣan'ā' named Al-Qullays.
  • It was constructed with colored marble, gold, and silver to dazzle the Arabs.
  • Goal: To replace the Ka'bah as the center of pilgrimage.
