The Irony of Isāf and Nā'ilah

From Divine Punishment to Pagan Worship
Isāf
إساف
Nā'ilah
نائلة
1

The Transgression

Two lovers from the Jurhum tribe who committed adultery inside the sacred precincts of the Ka'bah, violating its sanctity.

2

The Punishment

As a divine sign, they were instantly petrified (turned to stone). The people placed them at Safa and Marwah as a warning to others.

3

The Corruption

Over centuries, the "warning" narrative was lost. Later generations assumed these statues were placed there to be venerated, turning sinners into idols.

Placed at Al-Ṣafā and Al-Marwah, they became central to the corrupted Hajj rites of Quraysh.
