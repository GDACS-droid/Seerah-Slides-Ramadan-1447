Rebuilding the Ka'bah: The Crisis

Flood, Decay, and Divine Providence
The Structural Decay
  • Vulnerable Structure: The walls were loose stones above a man's height, with no roof or clay mortar.
  • Theft: The lack of a roof allowed thieves to steal treasures kept inside the well of the Ka'bah.
  • The Flood: A massive torrent (Sayl) rushed down to the sanctuary, cracking the walls and making collapse imminent.
The Providential Means
  • The Shipwreck: A Roman merchant ship wrecked at the nearby port of Shu'aybah.
  • The Material: Quraysh purchased the wood from the ship to use for the Ka'bah's roof.
  • The Expert: A Coptic carpenter named Bāqūm agreed to assist in the construction.
The Psychological Barrier
The tribes were terrified to demolish the House of Allah, fearing a fate similar to Abraha's army. They hesitated until Al-Walīd ibn al-Mughīrah took the first axe, declaring:
"O Allah, we do not intend anything but good."
