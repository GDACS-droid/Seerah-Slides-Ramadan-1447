The Order of Hijrah

هِجْرَة المُسْلِمِين — Muḥarram to Rabī' al-Awwal, 1 AH (July–September 622 CE)
Early Migrants
Abū Salamah ibn 'Abd al-Asad Among the very first
'Āmir ibn Rabī'ah & wife Early wave
'Abdullāh ibn Jaḥsh With his family
'Umar ibn al-Khaṭṭāb Migrated openly, daring Quraysh
Ṣuhayb al-Rūmī Gave up all his wealth
The Final Departures
Abū Bakr al-Ṣiddīq Waited — told "perhaps Allah will give you a companion"
Prophet Muḥammad ﷺ Among the last to leave — 1 Rabī' al-Awwal
'Alī ibn Abī Ṭālib Stayed behind to return trusts (amānāt)
Timeline
Migration period ~2.5 months in small groups
Prophet's departure 1 Rabī' al-Awwal (~13 Sept 622)
Arrival at Qubā' 8 Rabī' al-Awwal (~20 Sept 622)
The Prophet ﷺ was among the last to leave Makkah, ensuring that the vulnerable had departed safely first. Quraysh, realizing the exodus, plotted to assassinate him before he could leave — leading to the dramatic night of the Hijrah.
