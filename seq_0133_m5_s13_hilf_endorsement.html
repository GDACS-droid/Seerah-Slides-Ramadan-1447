Ḥilf al-Fuḍūl: The Prophetic Endorsement

"I Would Not Exchange It for Red Camels"
"I witnessed in the house of 'Abdullāh ibn Jud'ān a pact... I would not exchange my part in it for red camels. If I were called to it in Islam, I would respond."
— Prophet Muḥammad ﷺ (Musnad Aḥmad)
The Cultural Idiom
"Red Camels" (Ḥumr al-Na'am) represented the absolute peak of wealth and prestige in Arabian society. By using this metaphor, he elevated moral justice above material value.
The Ethical Principle
This endorsement establishes that Islam does not reject pre-existing good. Ethical frameworks are valid regardless of their origin if they align with justice and divine law.
