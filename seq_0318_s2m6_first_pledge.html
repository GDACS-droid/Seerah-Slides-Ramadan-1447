The First Pledge of 'Aqabah

بَيْعَة العَقَبَة الأُولَى — Year 12 of Prophethood (621 CE)
Bay'at al-Nisā' (The Women's Pledge)
12 men — 10 from Khazraj, 2 from Aws
  • To worship Allah alone (Tawḥīd) and associate nothing with Him
  • To refrain from theft, adultery, and the killing of children
  • To refrain from slander and false accusation
  • To obey the Prophet ﷺ in all that is good (ma'rūf)
Called "Bay'at al-Nisā'" because it contained no military clause — the same terms later given to women at the Conquest of Makkah.
Strategic Outcome
The Prophet ﷺ sent Muṣ'ab ibn 'Umayr to Yathrib as the first Muslim ambassador and teacher. He was to recite the Qur'ān, teach the faith, and prepare the ground for what would come next.
Significance
For the first time, the Prophet ﷺ had a formal political alliance outside of Makkah. The da'wah was no longer confined to Quraysh territory.
