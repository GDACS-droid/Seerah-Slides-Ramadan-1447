Isnād Analysis: The Baḥīrā Incident

Source Criticism (Taḥqīq)
The Narration (Isnād)
Reported by Al-Tirmidhī
Classified as Ḥasan Gharīb (Good but strange/isolated).
The Narrator Issue
The chain includes 'Abd al-Raḥmān ibn Ghazwān, whom some scholars consider disputed or prone to errors in details.
Scholarly Opinions
Ibn Kathīr
Accepted the core narrative of the journey but cautioned about specific miraculous details.
Al-Dhahabī
Expressed reservations about elements that seemed contradictory to established Prophetic timelines.
The Academic Position
Historical Plausibility
The core event (a journey to Syria with his uncle) is historically plausible and widely transmitted.
Methodological Lesson
Not every popular Seerah story has a Ṣaḥīḥ (Authentic) chain. We distinguish between "proven fact" and "narrative tradition."
