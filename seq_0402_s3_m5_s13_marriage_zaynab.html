Social Reform: Zaynab bint Jaḥsh

The Pre-Islamic Taboo

In Jāhiliyyah, an adopted son (like Zayd ibn Ḥārithah) was treated exactly as a biological son in lineage and inheritance. Consequently, it was considered incestuous for a father to marry his adopted son's ex-wife.

The Situation: Zayd and Zaynab's marriage was fraught with incompatibility and ended in divorce.

The Legislative Command

To shatter this false social construct, Allāh commanded the Prophet ﷺ to marry Zaynab. This was a heavy burden for the Prophet ﷺ due to the social stigma, but necessary for law-making.

"We married her to you in order that there not be upon the believers any discomfort concerning the wives of their adopted sons when they no longer have need of them."

Surah Al-Aḥzāb (33:37)
Legal Principle: Adoption is caretaking (Kafālah), not lineage (Nasab). Blood ties must be preserved truthfully.
