Analytical: Economic Independence

The End of Poverty for the Muhājirūn

"We never ate our fill of dates until we conquered Khaybar."

— 'Abdullāh ibn 'Umar

Financial Dignity

For six years, the Muhājirūn relied on the support of the Anṣār. Khaybar provided them with independent assets and wealth for the first time since leaving Makkah.

Repaying the Anṣār

The Prophet ﷺ instructed the Muhājirūn to return the date palms that the Anṣār had generously shared with them upon their arrival, restoring the Anṣār's full property.

State Treasury

The 50% annual harvest share from Khaybar created a steady revenue stream, allowing the state to equip armies for Mu'tah and the Conquest of Makkah without depleting personal wealth.
