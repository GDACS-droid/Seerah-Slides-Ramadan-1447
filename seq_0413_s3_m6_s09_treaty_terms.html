The Treaty of Ḥudaybiyyah

The Compromise: Suhayl ibn 'Amr refused to write "Rasūlullāh" (Messenger of Allāh). When 'Alī refused to erase it, the Prophet ﷺ erased it with his own hand and wrote "Muḥammad ibn 'Abdullāh" to secure the peace.
1

10-Year Truce

A complete cessation of hostilities for ten years. This granted the Muslims security to spread Islam without the threat of war.

2

The "Unfair" Clause

Any man who comes to Muhammad from Quraysh without permission must be returned. But any Muslim who flees to Quraysh need not be returned.

3

Deferment of 'Umrah

The Muslims must return to Madīnah this year. They can perform 'Umrah next year, staying in Makkah for only three days with sheathed swords.

4

Freedom of Alliance

Any tribe is free to ally with Muhammad or with Quraysh. (This allowed Khuzā'ah to ally with the Muslims and Banū Bakr with Quraysh).

Political Victory: By signing a treaty, Quraysh officially recognized the Islamic State as a sovereign equal power for the first time.
