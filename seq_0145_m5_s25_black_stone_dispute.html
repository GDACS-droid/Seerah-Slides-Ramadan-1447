The Black Stone Dispute

A Crisis of Honor
When construction reached the corner of the Black Stone (Al-Ḥajar al-Aswad), a fierce dispute erupted. Every clan wanted the eternal honor of placing the sacred stone in its final position.
The Bowl of Blood Pact
Banū 'Abd al-Dār brought a bowl filled with blood. They and Banū 'Adī dipped their hands into it—a solemn pre-Islamic ritual (La'qat al-Dam) signifying a pledge to fight to the death rather than yield the honor.
"Work halted for four or five days. Swords were sharpened, and Makkah stood on the brink of a devastating civil war."
لَعْقَةُ الدَّم La'qat al-Dam (The Licking of Blood Pact)
