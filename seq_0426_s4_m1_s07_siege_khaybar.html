The Siege of Khaybar

A Campaign Against Impregnable Fortresses

Khaybar was not a single city, but a vast oasis containing independent, heavily fortified citadels built on volcanic rock, separated by palm groves.

Al-Naṭāt

The First Line of Defense

  • Fort Na'im: The strategic entry point.
  • Fort Sa'b: The food depot; its capture saved the army from starvation.
  • Scene of the fiercest fighting.

Al-Shiqq

The Middle Defense

  • Fort Ubayy: Heavily defended.
  • Fort Al-Sumran: The defenders fled here after Ubayy fell.
  • Systematic clearing of resistance.

Al-Katībah

The Last Stand

  • Fort Al-Qāmūṣ: The massive citadel of Ibn Abi al-Huqayq.
  • Al-Waṭīḥ & Al-Sulālim: The final points of surrender.
  • Impregnable without a siege.
