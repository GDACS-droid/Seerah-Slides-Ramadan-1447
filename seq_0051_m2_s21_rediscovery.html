The Rediscovery of Zamzam

Excavation, Dispute, and Vindication

The Lonely Excavation

'Abd al-Muṭṭalib dug for three days with only his son Al-Ḥārith to protect him. The Quraysh mocked them, refusing to help disturb the sacred enclosure.

The Treasure of Jurhum

Upon striking the well cover, he found the buried wealth of the ancient tribe:

  • 2 Gold Gazelles
  • Ancient Armor
  • Swords
The Dispute

Quraysh demanded a share: "This is the well of our father Ismā'īl." They challenged his sole ownership of the water and the treasure.

The Dedication

After divine vindication, 'Abd al-Muṭṭalib melted the gold and hung the swords on the Ka'bah door—the first to adorn the House with gold.

"Takbīr! It is the well of Ismā'īl!" — 'Abd al-Muṭṭalib's cry upon finding water.
