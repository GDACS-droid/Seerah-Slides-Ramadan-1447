The Battle of Bu'āth

يَوْمُ بُعَاث — c. 617 CE (~5 years before the Hijrah)
The Conflict
The final major battle between Al-Aws and Al-Khazraj, fought southeast of Yathrib. Decades of blood feuds culminated in this devastating clash. Jewish tribes were drawn in on opposing sides: Banū Qurayẓah with Al-Aws, Banū Qaynuqā' and Banū Naḍīr with Al-Khazraj.
Al-Aws, initially losing, rallied and won — but the victory was pyrrhic. Both sides suffered heavy casualties, and many tribal leaders were killed, creating a leadership vacuum.
Why It Matters
Bu'āth exhausted both tribes militarily and psychologically. The people of Yathrib were desperate for a neutral arbiter who could end the cycle of violence. This exhaustion made them uniquely receptive to the Prophet's message — not just as a spiritual guide, but as a political unifier.
The Prophet ﷺ later said that Allah had prepared the ground through Bu'āth for the acceptance of Islam in Yathrib.
"Allah sent me at a time when the people of Yathrib were most in need of unity."
— Prophetic wisdom on the timing of the Da'wah in Yathrib
