The Night Before Battle

The Muslim Camp
Beneficial Rain

A light rain fell, firming the sandy ground for better footing and purifying the believers for prayer.

Divine Slumber (Nu'ās)

A supernatural sleep overtook the army, removing fear and refreshing them for the day ahead.

Tranquility (Sakīnah)

Despite being outnumbered 3 to 1, the camp was calm, disciplined, and spiritually prepared.

The Quraysh Camp
Torrential Rain

Heavy rain turned their ground into mud, hindering movement and adding to their discomfort.

Internal Dissension

'Utbah ibn Rabī'ah argued to return home, but Abu Jahl shamed him, forcing the army into an unwanted war.

Anxiety & Arrogance

Driven by pride but plagued by bad omens and the fear of fighting their own kinsmen.

"[Remember] when He overwhelmed you with drowsiness [giving] security from Him and sent down upon you from the sky, rain..." (Sūrah al-Anfāl 8:11)
